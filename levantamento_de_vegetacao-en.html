Vegetation Studies


Vegetation Studies provide essential information for understanding the composition, structure, and dynamics of plant communities in a given area. These studies support environmental licensing processes, monitoring programs, and conservation planning, especially in projects that involve intervention in native vegetation.

They may be required as part of Environmental Impact Assessments (EIA), Preliminary Environmental Reports (RAP), or Simplified Environmental Reports (RAS), during the preliminary licensing or project feasibility stages. Vegetation studies are also essential in the monitoring phases of project implementation and operation, helping to assess and mitigate potential impacts on natural habitats and forest resources.



Services Offered:

  • Vegetation Characterization and Structural Classification

  • Identification and Mapping of Vegetation Strata and Successional Stages

  • Survey, Inventory, and Monitoring of Native and Secondary Forests

  • Floristic and Phytosociological Surveys

  • Assessment of Areas of Permanent Preservation (APP) and Legal Reserves

  • Evaluation of Vegetation Suppression and Regeneration Potential

  • Monitoring of Vegetation Cover and Habitat Recovery

  • Leaf Morphometry Analysis

  • Carbon Credit Program Development

  • Support for Atlantic Forest Municipal Plan (PMMA) Initiatives;

  • Terrestrial and Aquatic Flora Bioprospecting.

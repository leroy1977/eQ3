Environmental Compensation and Recovery


Depending on the type and scale of an enterprise, it may be necessary to establish Environmental Commitment Terms to address and minimize the environmental liabilities generated by human activities — whether industrial, commercial, rural, or residential.

The Environmental Recovery Commitment Term (TCRA) is a formal agreement between the involved parties — such as CETESB, the Biodiversity Inspection Coordination, and the executor — in which the entrepreneur commits to restore or compensate for environmental impacts and damages resulting from their activities. In the state of São Paulo, this process follows specific legal and technical requirements to ensure environmental compliance and restoration effectiveness.

At eQuanta, we provide specialized guidance on environmental legislation and design compensation and recovery plans that are efficient, cost-effective, and tailored to each project. Our goal is to achieve full legal compliance while maximizing environmental value and contributing to the long-term resilience and valuation of the property or enterprise.

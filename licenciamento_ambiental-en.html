Environmental Licensing


Why Do We Need an Environmental License? Why Preserve Nature?

Investing in biodiversity conservation creates tangible value for your enterprise. Beyond protecting natural habitats, these areas provide essential ecosystem services that sustain human well-being and economic activity. Air and water purification, climate regulation, erosion control, natural pest management, and flood prevention are examples of environmental services offered free of charge by forests, rivers, and oceans.

To ensure the conservation of these ecosystems, Brazil maintains a solid framework of environmental legislation. Compliance is monitored by municipal, state, and federal environmental agencies, and supported by environmental police forces. Failure to comply can result in fines, work stoppages, or even detention. In addition to legal penalties, noncompliance damages corporate reputation and compromises long-term sustainability - with consequences that extend to future generations.

With the support of eQuanta’s qualified environmental professionals, your enterprise can align economic development with the preservation of natural resources — adding value to your project and contributing to a better quality of life for all.

Which Areas Should Be Preserved?
In the State of São Paulo and other regions of Brazil, except in special cases, it is not permitted to:

- Intervene or build in Permanent Preservation Areas (APP);
- Clear forests in medium or advanced stages of regeneration;
- Impermeabilize soil beyond limits established by law, particularly in Water Source Protection and Recovery Areas (APRM).

Legislation also requires that 20% of rural property, excluding APPs, be designated as a Legal Reserve Area (ARL), registered in the Rural Environmental Registry (CAR). This registration is mandatory during the vegetation clearing authorization process and essential for project licensing and compliance.

What Is the Purpose of the CAR?
The Rural Environmental Registry (CAR) is a mandatory electronic system for all rural properties in Brazil. It integrates key environmental information about:

- Permanent Preservation Areas (APP)
- Legal Reserve Areas (ARL)
- Forests and remnants of native vegetation
- Areas of restricted use
- Consolidated rural areas
CAR serves as the foundation for environmental control, planning, and restoration at the national level.
